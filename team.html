// Section 02 — Role & Contribution Model

Brand, Growth & Business Development Associate — Four Contribution Lanes

As a Brand, Growth & Business Development Associate, compensation is determined by which contribution lane is being worked in and the nature of the commercial result generated. The four lanes are independent but may overlap on certain engagements. Each has its own compensation structure.

A. Client Acquisition / Business Development

This lane covers all work related to identifying, approaching, pitching, closing, and managing client relationships for paid engagements delivered by The Shriks. Your compensation in this lane is commission-based and tied directly to attributable client acquisition.

Role Definition Commission %
Cold Outreach — Lead Only The associate identifies and contacts the prospect. The Shriks team handles pitch, close, and delivery. 5% – 7%
Cold Outreach — Full Close The associate identifies, cold contacts, pitches, and closes the client independently or with minimal venture closing support. 15% – 17%
End-to-End Acquisition The associate manages the acquisition process end-to-end — from identifying the opportunity and initiating contact through pitching and securing the client engagement. Ongoing communication may continue, but the commission band is based on end-to-end acquisition ownership, not simply relationship maintenance alone. 15% – 20%
Warm Referral The associate introduces an existing contact or warm lead who then engages The Shriks. No full outbound sales process is involved. 5% – 8%

Important: The exact percentage within the applicable range must be confirmed in writing by the founding team before any outreach begins. Commission applies only to work where client acquisition is reasonably attributable to the effort. If there is also contribution to delivery on the same engagement, both BD commission and delivery compensation may apply — see dual-role rules in Section 05.

B. Venture Growth / Brand Marketing

This lane covers work that builds The Shriks' brand presence, visibility, and commercial positioning. This includes founder content support, social media management, reels and posts, website and documentation content, awareness campaigns, and venture positioning work.

Compensation structure: This work is not tied to direct client revenue and therefore is not compensated through commission. Instead, it may be compensated through milestone-based bonuses, capped revenue-linked incentives where a commercial result is reasonably attributable, or — where venture cash flow permits — a fixed stipend or retainer component.

What this is not: This is not equity. This is not company ownership. This is not permanent product share. This is contribution-based compensation tied to defined milestones or attributable commercial growth.

Initial-stage reality: In the early phase of venture growth, no fixed stipend is guaranteed for this lane unless separately agreed in writing. Compensation may rely on milestone bonuses or discretionary incentives until venture cash flow supports a more structured model.

C. Product Launch / Product Distribution

This lane covers work related to launching, distributing, and promoting venture products — including LokiAI and future product releases. This includes launch content, reels, posts, waitlist campaigns, distribution strategy, launch assets, and product visibility work.

Compensation structure: Work in this lane may be compensated through a capped revenue-linked incentive tied to a defined launch window or campaign period, milestone-based bonuses for launch execution, or a hybrid model combining both.

What "share" means here: "Share" refers only to an agreed share of revenue generated within a defined launch or commercial window. It does not create ownership, equity, product rights, or permanent entitlement to future product revenue. The incentive structure, cap, and attribution model must be confirmed in writing before the campaign or launch begins.

Attribution requirement: Revenue-linked incentives apply only where the commercial result is reasonably attributable to your launch or distribution effort. Where attribution is unclear, milestone-based compensation may be used instead.

D. Client Delivery Marketing

This lane covers digital marketing, content execution, growth campaigns, and launch support delivered as a paid service to a client. This is execution work delivered under The Shriks venture. Examples include digital marketing packages, social media management for clients, campaign execution, and growth strategy implementation.

Compensation structure: If the digital marketing work is executed for a client engagement, separately agreed digital marketing execution compensation may be received for that specific scope. This is separate from BD commission. If the client was both acquired and the marketing work is being executed, both compensation categories may apply.

Important: For standard technical projects (software, systems, web, AI, automation), The Shriks venture delivers the project. The primary compensation on such projects is commercial acquisition commission unless a specific execution role has been separately agreed. General project delivery share is not received simply for bringing in the client — delivery remains with The Shriks venture.

Revenue from client engagements is allocated across defined categories. The allocation model may vary depending on project type, team size, scope, delivery involvement, business development role, and venture operational needs. There is no universal fixed formula that applies to every engagement.

Early-stage reference model: For small-team projects in the initial phase, a common allocation framework may resemble the following structure. This is not a permanent law — it is a reference point that may be adjusted based on the engagement.

Final allocation for each engagement must be agreed in advance and confirmed in writing where compensation is tied to that engagement.

Important: Delivery allocation is not always 60%. Venture overhead is not always 20%. BD commission is not always 20%. These percentages may shift based on the nature of the engagement, the team involved, and the commercial structure of the project.

Payment Terms & Venture Commitments

  • 5.1

  • 5.2

It does not automatically apply to subsequent invoices, retainer renewals, or project extensions unless separately confirmed in writing by the founding team.
  • 5.5
    Payment within 7 working days. Commission and delivery compensation are disbursed within 7 working days of The Shriks receiving cleared client funds for the relevant invoice. Milestone bonuses and launch incentives are disbursed within 7 working days of milestone completion or campaign period close, subject to revenue receipt where applicable.

  • 5.7
    Dual role — BD and delivery. If the same individual who brought in the client also contributes to technical or marketing delivery, both BD commission and proportionate share of the delivery allocation are received. Both are calculated independently and paid together.
  • 5.8
    Written confirmation required before work begins. The applicable commission percentage, delivery share, milestone bonus structure, or revenue-linked incentive model must be confirmed in writing — email, message, or signed acknowledgement — by a founding team member before any outreach, campaign, or delivery work begins. Verbal agreements are not binding under this policy.
  • 5.9
    Project cancellation or client non-payment. If a client cancels before paying, or a payment is not recoverable, no commission or revenue-linked incentive is owed for the unpaid amount. The Shriks holds no liability to pay compensation from studio funds in such circumstances. If partial payment is received, compensation is calculated on the amount actually received.
  • 5.10
    Initial-stage cash constraint. In the initial phase of studio growth, The Shriks may not be able to provide fixed monthly stipend or salary for this role. Compensation in this stage may rely on client commission, delivery compensation, milestone bonuses, and capped launch or product revenue-linked incentives. As studio cash flow grows, the compensation structure may evolve to include stipend, retainer, or monthly fixed components.
  • 5.11
    Attribution requirement for revenue-linked incentives. Revenue-linked incentives apply only where the commercial result is reasonably attributable to the effort. Attribution may be based on direct lead source, outreach trail, campaign ownership, tracked link or code, clearly approved launch or distribution responsibility, or founder/team written confirmation. Where attribution is unclear, disputed, or not reasonably provable, The Shriks may instead use milestone-based bonus or an agreed discretionary incentive.
  • 5.12
    Policy amendments and team growth. This policy may be updated by the founding team with reasonable advance notice to active contributors. As more people join and the studio grows, terms may evolve, percentages may change, and project allocation models may be revised. Changes will be discussed in advance with the active team and communicated before applying to new work. Existing ongoing engagements will continue under the previously agreed terms unless all relevant parties agree otherwise in writing.
// Section 06 — Definition of "Share"

What "Share" Means In This Document

"Share" in this document does not mean equity, ownership, partnership interest, company stake, or permanent product ownership.

It refers only to a defined and agreed share of revenue generated from an attributable client engagement, campaign, launch window, product distribution effort, or commercial result, as specifically confirmed in writing before the work begins.

Revenue-linked incentives tied to product launches or campaigns are capped, time-bound, and apply only within the defined commercial window. They do not create ongoing entitlement to future product revenue, equity in the product, or ownership rights of any kind.

This section exists to remove ambiguity completely. If you are told you will receive "a share" of a launch or campaign, that means only a share of revenue generated within the agreed period and cap — nothing more.

// Section 07 — Attribution Rules

How Attribution Is Determined

Revenue-linked incentives — whether for client acquisition, product launches, or campaigns — apply only where the commercial result is reasonably attributable to the effort. Attribution is not automatic. It must be provable or agreed.

Attribution may be based on:

  • 7.1
    Direct lead source — you initiated the contact, outreach, or referral that led to the engagement.
  • 7.2
    Outreach trail — documented evidence of your pitch, communication, or relationship management with the client.
  • 7.3
    Campaign ownership — you were assigned and executed a defined launch, distribution, or growth campaign.
  • 7.4
    Tracked link, code, or form — revenue can be traced to a specific campaign asset you managed.
  • 7.5
    Clearly approved launch or distribution responsibility — written responsibility was given for a product launch or distribution effort.
  • 7.6
    Founder or team written confirmation — the founding team confirms in writing that the result is attributable to the work.

Where attribution is unclear, disputed, or not reasonably provable, The Shriks may instead use milestone-based bonus or an agreed discretionary incentive rather than revenue-linked compensation. This protects both the associate and the studio from disputes over unclear commercial results.
